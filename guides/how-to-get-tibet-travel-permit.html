What is a Tibet Travel Permit?

The Tibet Travel Permit (TTP) is an official document issued by the Tibet Tourism Bureau.

It is the core document for traveling within Tibet.

Important

You cannot apply for a TTP individually. It must be processed through a licensed Tibet travel agency. TibetRide handles all permit applications free of charge for guests who book our tours.

Who Needs a Tibet Travel Permit?

The following travelers are required to obtain a TTP before entering Tibet:

  • All foreign passport holders
  • Taiwan residents

Mainland Chinese citizens, Hong Kong residents, and Macao residents do not need a TTP.

How to Apply

Applying for a TTP is a simple three-step process:

1

Book a Tibet Tour

Choose any TibetRide tour and confirm your booking. We can only start the permit process after your tour is confirmed.

2

Submit Required Documents

Send us a scan of your passport bio page and China visa page. Taiwan residents should send their Mainland Travel Permit.

3

Wait for Approval & Receive Your Permit

We will process your application within 7-15 working days. The original permit will be delivered to your hotel in China, or our staff will hand it to you at the airport or train station.

Processing Time & Cost

For guests booking a TibetRide tour, the TTP application is completely free. Processing time varies by season:

  • Peak season (May-Oct): 10-15 working days
  • Low season (Nov-Apr): 7-10 working days
  • Urgent application: 3-5 working days (extra fee applies)
Tip

We recommend confirming your booking and submitting documents at least 30 days in advance to ensure ample processing time.

After You Receive the Permit

Once you receive your TTP, please keep the following in mind:

  • You must present the original permit when boarding flights or trains. Digital copies are not accepted.
  • Carry your TTP with you at all times while traveling in Tibet. Police and checkpoints will check it frequently.
  • The itinerary on your TTP must match your actual route. Notify your agency in advance if you need to change your plans.
  • When leaving Lhasa for other regions, you may also need an Aliens' Travel Permit and Military Area Entry Permit.

FAQ

Q1: Can I apply for a TTP by myself in Lhasa?

No. Individuals cannot apply for a TTP directly. It must be processed through a licensed Tibet travel agency.

Q2: How much does a TTP cost?

For TibetRide guests, the TTP is completely free. Market rates for standalone applications typically range from USD 50-100.

Q3: Can I change my travel route?

Yes, but you must notify us in advance to update the itinerary on your permit. Mismatched routes may result in being denied passage at checkpoints.

Q4: How long is the TTP valid?

The TTP is only valid for your specified travel dates, typically the start and end dates of your tour. It cannot be used for other dates or routes.
