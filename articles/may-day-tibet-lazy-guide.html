01

看《文成公主》实景剧

▲图源丨文成公主大型实景剧

去看《文成公主》大型实景剧，剧场建在海拔超3700米的宝瓶山上，别看是史诗剧，你只用负责坐着震撼！

▲图源丨文成公主大型实景剧

总计约90分钟的演出，数百人极具视觉冲击力的庞大阵容，仿佛穿越了1300年前的历史长卷。

▲图源丨文成公主大型实景剧

（小贴士看剧记得带件厚衣服，拉萨的晚风有点“霸道”）

02

坐船夜游拉萨

▲图源丨情满拉萨河

去体验 吉尼斯认证的“最高海拔夜游” ——夜游拉萨河，这项特色玩法，更是成功入选马蜂窝《2026中国文旅新玩法报告》TOP100榜单！

▲图源丨情满拉萨河

在游船的甲板上，吹着高原凉爽的晚风，看城市建筑的轮廓倒映在河面上，两岸的声光电喷泉秀与民族舞蹈接连上演。关键是不用走路！全程约30-60分钟。

▲图源丨情满拉萨河

03

搭乘美食主题观光巴士

▲图源丨拉萨文旅

拉萨美食主题观光巴士串起了布达拉宫、拉萨河等核心地标，你只需要坐在座位上，就能欣赏流动的拉萨风光。

▲图源丨拉萨文旅

车上提供有下午茶/晚餐，边吃边喝边看美景！

（微信小程序搜“拉萨双层美食主题观光巴士”就能预约）

04

逛西藏博物馆

▲图源丨小红书@张小川在拍照

走进这座位于罗布林卡路的一级博物馆，这里分分钟让你穿越雪域高原的千年记忆。

▲图源丨小红书@张小川在拍照

强烈推荐直奔二楼的“雪域长歌”历史展，必看镇馆之宝“ 双体陶罐 ”，旁边还有数不清的精美唐卡、神奇藏医药和色彩斑斓的民俗服饰馆。

（注意：免费！提前1-7天在公众号或官网预约！刷身份证入馆。周一闭馆免跑空！）

05

体验唐卡绘画、藏香制作

▲图源丨小红书@赤烈📷

在遍布八廓街的 艺术工坊 ，做一次“非遗手艺人”！找一个藏香制作体验课，亲手将香料制成线香，带回西藏特有的草木芳香。

▲图源丨小红书@赤烈📷

或者在唐卡画室里，体验藏地细腻的矿物颜料与极致的笔触美学，做个半日“神笔马良”。不仅不累，还自带治愈性舒缓解压。

06

参观藏毯厂

▲图源丨拉萨文旅

在西郊的喀瓦坚手工藏毯厂，

你可以慢慢穿梭在光影斑驳的编织车间，近距离看手艺人们用传统手工打结法，看五颜六色的羊毛线在巧手中变成精美的藏毯，感受传统手工的温度，沉浸式体会岁月沉淀的匠心与美好。

▲图源丨拉萨文旅

（去之前需要和藏毯厂提前预约哦。）

07

体验藏药浴

▲图源丨摄影师/胡王光

走了一天再也不怕脚酸！安排一场藏医药浴，选用天然药材，38-42℃的水温刚好舒缓疲劳，祛风活血，泡20分钟再裹上毛巾发汗，浑身都通透，完美缓解旅途疲惫。

▲图源丨摄影师/胡王光

（初次体验建议只泡脚。）

08

罗布林卡草坪躺平

▲图源丨文化西藏

罗布林卡藏语意为 “宝贝园林” ，在“拉萨的颐和园”的草坪上躺平才是正经事！

▲图源丨摄影师/李珩

挑个午后，带本书、带块野餐布，在苍松翠柏间就地一躺！四周花木错落、绿意盎然，雅致亭台与清幽水榭相映成趣，清风拂面，满目清新。

▲图源丨文化西藏

09

甜茶馆和当地人闲聊

▲图源丨摄影师/襢思

去 老光明岗琼甜茶馆 坐一坐，本地人密度极高！在桌上放好零钱，等着服务员穿梭倒茶，一块钱一杯的热甜茶喝到爽。

▲图源丨摄影师/襢思

再来一碗牛肉藏面、一块炸土豆，周围人声鼎沸，看藏族阿妈和老友谈笑风生，这种人间烟火气的市井氛围感狠狠拿捏！

▲图源丨摄影师/襢思

10

喝一杯藏式咖啡

▲图源丨小红书@藏熹

拉萨是 中国咖啡浓度最高的城市 ，拉萨咖啡馆密度全国第一，830家咖啡店融入大街小巷。

▲图源丨小红书@kamawen

走累了就去拉萨街头的咖啡店坐一坐，点一杯“ 青稞dirty ”，上层是焦香顺滑的咖啡浓缩，下层是冰爽甘冽的青稞酒酿，口感炸裂，

咖啡香里，混着高原独有的慵懒， 坐着发呆也是极致享受。

▲图源丨小红书@木里|旅居在拉萨

▲图源丨小红书@藏熹

May Day holiday countdown!

Want to visit Tibet but don't want a "special forces" style trip?

This May Day guide features 10 "lie-flat" ways to enjoy Lhasa.

For lazy travelers who don't like running around!

01 Watch the "Princess Wencheng" Live Show

Source: Princess Wencheng Large-Scale Live Show

Go watch the "Princess Wencheng" large-scale live show. The theater is built on Baoping Mountain at over 3,700 meters altitude. Despite being an epic production, you only need to sit back and be amazed!

The approximately 90-minute performance features hundreds of performers with visually stunning grandeur, as if traveling through a 1,300-year historical scroll.

(Tip: Remember to bring a thick coat. Lhasa's evening wind is a bit "overbearing.")

02 Night Cruise on the Lhasa River

Source: Love Fills the Lhasa River

Experience the Guinness-certified "Highest Altitude Night Cruise" — the Lhasa River night tour. This special experience was selected for Mafengwo's "2026 China Cultural Tourism New Experience Report" TOP100 list!

On the cruise deck, enjoy the cool plateau evening breeze while watching city building outlines reflected on the river. Sound and light fountain shows and ethnic dances perform continuously on both banks. The key point: no walking required! About 30-60 minutes total.

03 Food-Themed Sightseeing Bus

Source: Lhasa Cultural Tourism

The Lhasa food-themed sightseeing bus connects the Potala Palace, Lhasa River, and other core landmarks. You just sit in your seat and enjoy the flowing scenery of Lhasa.

The bus provides afternoon tea/dinner — eat, drink, and enjoy the beautiful views!

04 Visit the Tibet Museum

Source: Xiaohongshu @Zhang Xiaochuan Taking Photos

Enter this first-class museum on Norbulingka Road. Here you'll instantly travel through a thousand years of memory on the snowy plateau. Highly recommended: the "Snowland Long Song" history exhibition on the second floor. Must-see treasure: the "Double-Body Pottery Jar."

(Note: Free! Reserve 1-7 days in advance via official account or website. ID required for entry. Closed on Mondays!)

05 Experience Thangka Painting and Tibetan Incense Making

Source: Xiaohongshu @Chilie

In art workshops scattered around Barkhor Street, become an "intangible heritage craftsman" for a day! Find a Tibetan incense-making experience class and personally make line incense from spices, bringing back Tibet's unique herbal fragrance.

Or in a thangka studio, experience Tibetan mineral pigments and exquisite brushwork aesthetics, becoming a half-day "magic brush Ma Liang." Not only not tiring, but also inherently healing and stress-relieving.

06 Visit a Tibetan Carpet Factory

Source: Lhasa Cultural Tourism

At the Kawajian handmade Tibetan carpet factory in the western suburbs, slowly wander through the light-and-shadow-dappled weaving workshop. Watch up close as craftsmen use traditional hand-knotting methods, turning colorful wool threads into exquisite Tibetan carpets with skillful hands.

07 Experience Tibetan Medicinal Bath

Source: Photographer/Hu Wangguang

After a day of walking, no more sore feet! Arrange a Tibetan medicinal bath using natural herbs. The 38-42°C water temperature perfectly soothes fatigue, dispels wind, and activates blood circulation. Soak for 20 minutes then wrap in a towel to sweat — your whole body becomes clear, perfectly relieving travel exhaustion.

(First-time experience: foot soak only recommended.)

08 Lie Flat on the Norbulingka Lawn

Source: Culture Tibet

Norbulingka means "baby garden" in Tibetan. Lying flat on the lawn of "Lhasa's Summer Palace" is the serious business! Pick an afternoon, bring a book and a picnic cloth, and lie down among the pines and cypresses!

09 Chat with Locals at a Sweet Tea House

Source: Photographer/Tansi

Go sit at the Old Guangming Gangqiong Sweet Tea House — extremely high density of locals! Put change on the table and wait for servers to shuttle back and forth pouring tea. One yuan per cup of hot sweet tea, drink to your heart's content.

Add a bowl of beef Tibetan noodles and a piece of fried potato. The bustling atmosphere of this worldly marketplace is absolutely captivating!

10 Drink a Cup of Tibetan-Style Coffee

Source: Xiaohongshu @Zangxi

Lhasa is the city with the highest coffee concentration in China. With 830 coffee shops integrated into streets and alleys.

When tired, sit at a Lhasa street coffee shop and order a "Highland Dirty" — top layer is rich espresso, bottom layer is chilled highland barley wine. The taste explodes! In the coffee aroma mixed with the plateau's unique laziness, sitting in a daze is also an ultimate enjoyment.

See you in Lhasa this May Day! Let's casually "do nothing" together~
