1月主题

冰湖蓝冰+藏历新年预热

出行亮点 ：普莫雍错蓝冰童话、琼穆岗嘎雪山徒步、拉萨晒太阳

穿着建议 ：防风羽绒服+抓绒内胆+保暖帽+防滑登山鞋

贴士 ： 冬季为西藏旅游淡季，机票酒店价格优惠，布达拉宫免门票

蓝冰观赏需由当地向导带领，切勿自行上冰 下旬开始可体验藏历新年筹备氛围（2026年藏历新年为2月18日）

Let's Go to Tibet in 2026! Your Year-Round Travel Guide

Author: Xiao Zhuoma | Source: Cultural Tibet | Date: January 28, 2026

Some say Tibet needs to be visited four times — see peach blossoms in spring, escape the heat in summer, view colorful forests in autumn, and bask in the sun in winter. But I say Tibet is worth visiting twelve times, because every month in Tibet unfolds completely different scenery and stories.

❄️ Winter: Half Frozen, Half Festive

Many people fear Tibet's winter, not knowing that this is when it's closest to a fairy tale. The lake surface at the foot of Qungmu Gangga Snow Mountain freezes like colored glaze. On Puma Yumco, sheep herds walk across the ice as if treading on a sapphire mirror.

January — Theme: Blue Ice Lakes + Tibetan New Year Warm-up. Highlights: Puma Yumco blue ice, Qungmu Gangga snow mountain hike, basking in the sun in Lhasa. Winter is low season with favorable prices and free Potala Palace admission.

February — Theme: Tibetan New Year + Winter Festivals. Highlights: Lhasa Tibetan New Year, Barkhor Street city walk. Experience the "Gutu" dough ball ceremony.

🌸 Spring: Flowers Bloom on the Plateau

While the peaks of Nyenchen Tanglha are still covered in snow, Nyingchi's century-old wild peach trees are already in full bloom. Peach blossoms flow like rosy clouds, reflecting glaciers and rising with cooking smoke.

March — Theme: Peach Blossoms Begin to Bloom + Spring Plowing. Highlights: Nyingchi Peach Blossom Valley early bloom, Shannan field spring plowing ceremony.

April — Theme: Peach Blossoms at Full Bloom + Hot Springs. Highlights: Bomi Peach Blossom Valley, Gala Village Peach Blossom Festival, Markam Salt Field.

May — Theme: Rhododendrons in Bloom + Snow Mountains. Highlights: Shergyla Mountain rhododendrons, hike Guru Lake, hike Medog. Hiking season begins.

🌿 Summer: Greenness Soaks In

While the inland regions are steaming with heat, Tibet is welcoming its gentlest breath. Snowmelt water awakens boundless meadows, forests are lush, wild flowers cover the slopes.

June — Theme: New Green Grasslands. Highlights: Nagqu grassland, Gangyun Spruce Forest, Tashilhunpo Monastery.

July — Theme: Linka Festival + Matsutake Season. Highlights: Forest picnic, matsutake mushroom picking, Yarlung Tsangpo River hike. Temperature: 14-25C.

August — Theme: Shoton Festival + Horse Racing. Highlights: Lhasa Shoton Festival, Nagqu Horse Racing Festival, Gyantse Dama Festival.

🍂 Autumn: Layers of Color

If spring is light crimson and summer is emerald green, then autumn is Tibet's most vibrant oil painting. Nature seems to have overturned its palette here.

September — Theme: Autumn Colors Emerge + Harvest Festivals. Highlights: Ngari golden grassland, Shannan Ongkor (Harvest) Festival. Namcha Barwa has its highest exposure probability.

October — Theme: Layers of Color + Palace Repainting. Highlights: Lulang Forest Sea autumn colors, Namcha Barwa full view, Potala Palace repainting season.

November — Theme: Fewer People + Stargazing Season. Highlights: Everest Base Camp stargazing, Zanda Earth Forest sunset, hot springs. Best month for stargazing.

December — Theme: Blue Ice Returns + Time Stands Still. Highlights: Puma Yumco blue ice, Tibet Museum, temple New Year wishes.

2026, see you in Tibet! January's ice blue, April's peach pink, July's emerald green, October's golden yellow... Tibet's four seasons are more than just the flow of scenery. It tells us that life can be so simple, yet so abundant.
